Simulation

During my research, my coauthors and I have come with
new ideas for simulation of the cellular inftrastucture in a smart-city in large scaleg.
In this section I describe briefly the work done in:

1) A simulation framework based on empirical distributions for large-scale simulation and evaluating the performance of IoT applications

2) A new approach for parallel (multi-thread) computing for large-scaled cellular inftrastructres with mobility and handovers.


1) Parallel computing for large-scale simulation
of cellular netwroks with handovers

This research project presents a very original idea to reduce between 80% and 100% the number of stops in parallel or multi-threading computing to perform synchronization stops between the multiple-computing elements or threads, improving dramatically the efficiency of the parallel computing efforts

This is a great idea for systems that require co-sumulation of different levels or dimensions of a problem where the influence of one of the systems on the another one can be considered uni-directional.

1) Simulation framework for evaluation of IoT performance
in geographically distributed and LTE-dependent applications

This framework allows simulating the telecom performance of those applications without actually simulating in detail the telecom protocols at the MAC layer. The MAC layer is where it's detemined the establishments of connections between devices and cellular antennas
